# MATHutrice
# Context
First-year engineering students often struggle with mathematical foundations. Many of them lack personalized support when practicing exercises or revising key concepts. Traditional learning methods either provide static exercises or encourage students to rely too heavily on AI tools that directly give the answers, which can hinder the development of their own reasoning. 

## Why 
This project addresses two main questions:

- How can we provide personalized support in mathematics to first-year students?
- How can students learn with AI without delegating their cognitive reasoning to it?

The target users are:

- First-year students, who need adaptive practice and guidance
- Teachers, who want tools to help identify students’ weaknesses and support their learning process

Our goal is to build a system that encourages active learning, not passive answer consumption.

## The solution
Our product is a web-based learning platform powered by an AI tutoring system.

The platform assists students in learning mathematics by:

- Generating customized exercises based on specific mathematical concepts
- Identifying knowledge gaps in the student’s understanding
- Allowing students to request exercises on specific topics
- Helping students practice efficiently and revise fundamentals
- Analyzing student answers to understand mistakes and misconceptions
- Encouraging engagement and motivation in learning mathematics

Unlike many AI tools, the platform is designed not to give direct answers, but to support the learning process by guiding students through practice and analysis.

The objective is to help students think, practice, and improve, rather than outsource their reasoning to AI.

# Scope
## How (= the magic behind the solution)
This project will be an AI tool deployed as a website, coded mostly with Python. It will be capable of generating exercises without ever giving the answers, analyzing the answers given by the user and use them to be aware of the user's difficulties.
